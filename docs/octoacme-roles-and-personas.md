# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create intuitive interfaces, and ensure that products meet usability standards and accessibility requirements. They bridge the gap between user needs and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Define information architecture and user flows
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into design solutions
- Work with Developers to ensure design feasibility and implementation quality
- Participate in design reviews and incorporate feedback
- Define and maintain design systems and guidelines

### Goals
- Create products that are intuitive and accessible
- Reduce user friction and support costs through great design
- Ensure design consistency across products and platforms

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with cross-functional teams
- User research insights and findings
- Design documentation and component libraries
- Collaboration in sprint planning and refinement sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs, feature priorities, and acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Collaborate on design feasibility, implementation details, and component specifications
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability testing and accessibility validation
- **Stakeholders**: Present user research findings and design rationale

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define, track, and interpret metrics that measure project and product success. They provide data-driven insights to inform decision-making and optimize outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics
- Set up tracking and instrumentation for user behavior and system performance
- Analyze data and identify trends, patterns, and anomalies
- Create dashboards and reports for visibility across the organization
- Conduct impact analysis and A/B testing
- Support hypothesis validation and continuous improvement efforts

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across the organization
- Measure and demonstrate project impact and ROI
- Identify optimization opportunities through quantitative insights

### Typical Communication
- Metrics definitions and tracking documentation
- Weekly or milestone-based analytics reports
- Data dashboards and visualization tools
- Collaboration with Product and Project teams on KPI alignment

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics, conduct impact analysis, validate hypotheses
- **Project Managers**: Track delivery metrics and project health indicators
- **Developers**: Collaborate on instrumentation and data collection requirements
- **Stakeholders**: Present insights and business impact reports

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, automation, and tools that enable teams to develop, test, and deploy software reliably and at scale.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment infrastructure
- Automate testing, building, and deployment processes
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Manage infrastructure as code and configuration management
- Support incident response and troubleshooting
- Optimize performance, reliability, and cost of infrastructure
- Ensure security best practices and compliance

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize downtime and manual intervention
- Reduce deployment risk and enable rapid iteration

### Typical Communication
- Runbooks and operational documentation
- Deployment coordination and incident communications
- Infrastructure and pipeline improvements documentation
- Collaboration on performance and security requirements

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Support local development environments, CI/CD integration, deployment processes
- **QA/Testing**: Enable test automation and staging environments
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate deployment timing and rollback procedures
- **Project Managers**: Provide deployment status and infrastructure health visibility

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and execute software releases. They ensure smooth deployments, clear communication, and rapid recovery from issues.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release plans and schedules
- Coordinate between development, QA, DevOps, and stakeholder teams
- Manage release notes and documentation
- Execute deployments and monitor deployment health
- Implement and execute rollback procedures when needed
- Communicate release status and timeline to stakeholders and support teams
- Conduct post-release verification and learning reviews

### Goals
- Execute releases on schedule with minimal risk
- Ensure clear communication across all teams and stakeholders
- Enable rapid response to post-deployment issues

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and timeline documentation
- Pre-release checklists and go/no-go communications
- Deployment status and incident alerts
- Release notes and changelog documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on release readiness and timeline
- **Product Managers**: Communicate feature availability and business impact
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freeze, final testing, and deployment procedures
- **DevOps Engineers**: Execute deployments, manage infrastructure changes
- **QA/Testing**: Verify release readiness and coordinate smoke tests
- **Support/Customer Success**: Provide release information and support preparation

---

## Customer Support Leads

### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads manage support operations, collect customer feedback, and escalate critical issues upstream. They are the voice of the customer in product and project decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and categorize customer support tickets and issues
- Identify patterns, trends, and frequently reported problems
- Escalate critical bugs and production issues to development and project teams
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback for product improvement
- Collaborate on incident response and communication during outages
- Train and support support team members
- Communicate product updates and features to support resources

### Goals
- Reduce customer friction and improve satisfaction
- Enable rapid response to customer-impacting issues
- Surface customer insights to drive product decisions

### Typical Communication
- Customer issue reports and trend analysis
- Escalation notifications and incident updates
- Customer feedback summaries and feature requests
- Support documentation and training materials

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and feature request trends
- **Project Managers**: Escalate critical issues, provide customer impact context
- **Developers**: Report bugs, provide reproduction steps, participate in incident response
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborate on reproduction and verification of customer-reported issues
- **Release Managers**: Provide customer impact analysis for urgent fixes

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
